About RMIS

Founded in 1948, the University of Wisconsin-Madison's Risk Management & Insurance Society has worked for 70 years to provide opportunities for informed, thoughtful and enthusiastic students to learn about risk management and how it can be applied to help make the world a better place. Since our inception, we as Business Badgers have always held true to our university's most important and founding principle: our actions should serve to positively affect the lives of those who call our state, our country, and our world "home."

The RMI major encompasses a world-class education that prepares students to identify, analyze, and manage risks that are inherent in the operation of every profit and non-profit institution. The RMI major lends itself to a career in multiple industries, and is a popular double major for students in the Wisconsin School of Business. Additionally, there is now a certificate in RMI that allows students both inside and outside the school of business to explore risk management and insurance. The classwork for both major and certificate incorporates analytics, real-world applications, and conceptual components to prepare students for a successful career. Risk is involved in every decision and business endeavor, and having an understanding of how to approach risk is critical to success in every career path.
